Lot 13: Set of PZH Gouda vases – Zonda décor (c. 1922–1928)
This set comprises two hand-painted ceramic vases produced by the Plateelbakkerij Zuid-Holland (PZH) in Gouda, The Netherlands, decorated in the Zonda pattern.
The vases carry different shape numbers – model 1017 and model 1071– yet were intentionally designed and finished as a matching pair, a common and appreciated practice within PZH production.
Dating from the mid-1920s, the vases exemplify the transition from late Art Nouveau to early Art Deco, a period in which PZH drew strong inspiration from Middle Eastern and Persian art.
This so-called Rhodian-inspired décor is characterised by a deep dark ground enriched with stylised floral motifs in vibrant colours such as red, green, turquoise and ochre.
Both vases are entirely hand-painted and fully marked on the base with the distinctive Lazarus gate mark, the inscription Gouda Holland, the respective model numbers, the décor name Zonda, and the painter’s initial (F), confirming their authenticity and origin from the PZH workshops.
The use of two different yet complementary shapes adds visual rhythm and balance to the set. Individually elegant, the vases are particularly striking when displayed together, making them highly attractive to collectors of Gouda pottery and early 20th-century decorative arts.
